UTCal

Live on App Store

The calibration calculator built for UT and PAUT inspectors — not for someone who read about UT in a textbook.

Download on the App Store

Free features

Skip Distance & Beam Path

Enter angle, thickness, and velocity — get surface distance, leg count, and beam path. The core of every PAUT setup.

Snell's Law

Calculate refracted angle from incident angle and material velocities. Useful for wedge selection and mode verification.

Pipe Reference Chart

Lookup OD, wall thickness, and schedule for standard pipe sizes. Quick reference without digging through a handbook.

Code Library (Basic)

Reference acceptance criteria for common codes. Full code library unlocks with Pro.

Pro features One-time purchase

Beam Path Viewer

Interactive beam path diagram. Slide the probe position and watch the geometry update in real time.

Weld Overlay & Index Offset

Weld geometry visualization with index offset calculator — so you're scanning the right location, not guessing.

Near Field, Dead Zone & More

Near field distance, dead zone, transfer correction, and beam spread — the formulas you need between jobs.

AWS D1.1 Indication Rating

Full structural weld evaluation per AWS D1.1. Enter your dB readings and sound path, get the indication rating and accept/reject verdict.

Pipe Chart Pro

Scan length calculation, favorites, and one-tap scan setup directly from pipe data.

Full Code Library

Acceptance criteria for ASME, API 650/653, B31.1, B31.3, and AWS D1.1 — plus technique references.

Weld Builder

Save complete job setups — material, angle, frequency, wedge — and recall them on the next job.

Fracture Mechanics

Critical flaw size and ASME Section VIII flaw evaluation. For when the engineer needs the numbers now.

Apple Watch

Pipe chart lookup right on your wrist. Check OD and wall thickness without touching your phone mid-scan. Included with Pro.

Works the way the job works

No account. No internet required. Your setups stay on your device. UTCal was built by a practicing PAUT inspector — the workflow matches what actually happens in the field, not a product manager's idea of what UT looks like.
